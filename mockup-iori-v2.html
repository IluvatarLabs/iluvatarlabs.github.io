Iluvatar Open Research Initiative.

Open computational biology, run on public data, reviewed in public, free to validate.

We run computational biology projects on public data, publish every iteration as it happens, and invite the field to critique, extend, and validate.

Pipelines and data are public. Every project starts from a question a PI couldn't answer because of computational bandwidth. Marvin synthesizes the literature, integrates the relevant public datasets, and produces a full logic trail. Pipelines are reproducible end-to-end and licensed Apache 2.0.
Findings are reviewed before they ship. Iluvatar PhDs vet every output. Each claim carries an explicit confidence tier, including a tier for what we did not measure. Failures and downgrades log alongside positive results, with the same visibility.
Outputs belong to the commons. Hypotheses, evidence, datasets, and pipelines publish under open licenses. Iluvatar takes no IP on findings produced through the initiative. Marvin's underlying architecture and training stay commercial; everything Marvin produces is open.
Submitting PIs keep the science. Marvin runs the analysis on public data. You lead the project. Co-authorship follows on resulting publications. Wet-lab researchers who pick up a published hypothesis get the same offer, with credit logged via CRediT.

How it works.

01 Submit a question
A PI proposes a computational biology question that's answerable from existing public data. We accept submissions on a rolling basis and select cohorts quarterly. Submit a question →
02 Marvin runs the analysis
Literature synthesis across the relevant corpus. Dataset integration with quality control, batch correction, and cross-platform normalization. Hypothesis generation with a full logic trail. Every decision and parameter choice is logged.
03 PhDs vet
Internal review by Iluvatar's research team. Confidence tiers applied to every finding. Methodological concerns flagged and routed back into the next iteration. Rejected hypotheses are kept in the project's permanent record.
04 Publish open
Preprint to bioRxiv. Repository public on GitHub. Iterations continue. Anyone can extend the analysis, critique a hypothesis, or volunteer to validate one in the lab.
